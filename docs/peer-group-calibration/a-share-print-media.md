# 1. A-Share Print Media Peer-Group Review

## 1.1 Purpose

This group covers provincial publishing groups, textbook distribution, trade publishing, scientific publishing, digital reading, newspapers, and distressed media companies. Publishing licenses and regional textbook channels can be stable but usually do not create strong growth; retention should be limited to the strongest education, content, or science/academic platforms.

## 1.2 Retention Standard

1. Retain boundary companies when textbook/education distribution, content IP, academic/science publishing, or provincial education platform creates durable cash flow and difficult-to-replicate rights.
2. Reject ordinary provincial publishers, newspapers, trade publishers, digital-reading challengers, and weak media names when they lack national content, education platform, or technology/data moat.
3. Treat publishing as a slow-growth, license-protected cash-flow business rather than a high-compounding industry.

## 1.4 Sources

Aggregator company introductions are not treated as decisive evidence.
