# 1. A-Share Other Chemical Raw Materials Peer-Group Review

## 1.1 Purpose

This broad chemical raw-material group mixes coal-to-olefins, electronic gases, wet electronic chemicals, photoresist, microsphere chromatography materials, nuclear-technology materials, C2/C3/C4 petrochemicals, industrial gases, amines, silanes, catalysts, pigments, polystyrene, titanium dioxide, and ST chemical cases. The review separates process/platform and customer-qualified materials from ordinary commodity capacity.

## 1.2 Retention Standard

1. Retain direct-watch companies when they control hard-to-copy material platforms such as electronic specialty gases, wet electronic chemicals/photoresist, high-performance chromatography microspheres, specialty polymers, or low-cost integrated green chemical platforms.
2. Retain boundary cases when a company has a specific customer-qualified niche in nuclear technology, semiconductor gases, conductive carbon black, piperazine derivatives, light-hydrocarbon integration, electronic-grade chemicals, polymer stabilizers, molecular sieves, thermal-storage salts, green process technology, or electronic silane.
3. Reject commodity petrochemical, polystyrene, methanol, titanium dioxide, basic acids/alkalis, pigments, ordinary adhesives, weaker gas suppliers, ST cases, and duplicate chemical-capacity stories already covered by stronger retained companies.
4. Do not reject hard material platforms because current chemicals are cyclical, but require evidence that the normalized edge comes from process, purity, cost, customer qualification, or resource/integration advantages.

Aggregator company introductions are not treated as decisive evidence.
