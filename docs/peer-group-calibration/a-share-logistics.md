# 1. A-Share Logistics Peer-Group Review

## 1.1 Purpose

This group contains integrated express networks, freight forwarding, chemical logistics, petrochemical storage, manufacturing logistics, commodity supply-chain trading, regional transport, warehousing equipment, and ST logistics cases. The review separates network density, safety qualification, infrastructure, and customer workflow moats from low-margin transport and trading capacity.

## 1.2 Retention Standard

1. Retain direct-watch companies when they own national or global logistics networks, premium express brands, air/cold-chain/supply-chain capability, or global freight-forwarding platform position.
2. Retain boundary cases when chemical/dangerous-goods logistics, petrochemical tank storage, cross-border infrastructure, road-port networks, or embedded manufacturing logistics create real customer switching costs.
3. Reject ordinary freight forwarding, contract logistics, regional transport, commodity supply-chain trading, local warehousing, weak express followers, and ST cases when a well-funded entrant or stronger retained platform can replicate or outscale the business.
4. Logistics licenses and warehouses are not enough; the company must show route/network density, customer workflow lock-in, safety qualification, infrastructure scarcity, or differentiated operating data.
