# 1. A-Share Internet Finance Peer-Group Review

## 1.1 Purpose

This group compares online wealth-management platforms, financial-data/terminal software, and fintech solution vendors. The review retains network/data/workflow moats and rejects generic financial IT projects.

## 1.2 Retention Standard

1. Retain direct-watch companies with large investor traffic, wealth-management transaction loops, financial information data, broker/fund-sales integration, or trading-workflow lock-in.
2. Retain boundary cases when a smaller financial-information platform has enough user workflow and product stickiness to deserve observation.
3. Reject fintech project vendors and weaker legacy terminals when they lack platform traffic, proprietary data/workflow lock-in, or clear differentiation from stronger retained peers.
4. Regulated finance adjacency alone is not enough; the company must own traffic, account relationships, data, workflow, or transaction infrastructure that capital cannot quickly reproduce.

## 1.4 Sources

This first-pass group review uses the local full-coverage score fields as background metadata and records official exchange disclosure landing pages plus official company websites in each decision row. Aggregator company introductions are not treated as decisive evidence. Later deep-company reviews should use annual reports, exchange announcements, official investor-relations materials, product certifications, customer qualification evidence, market-share evidence, segment margins, and reputable institutional research where needed.
