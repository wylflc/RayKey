# 1. A-Share Chemical New Materials Peer-Group Review

## 1.1 Purpose

This group compares insulation and optical films, biomass and phenolic/electronic materials, PVP specialty polymers, high-end ceramic materials, modified plastics, electromagnetic materials, thermal/EMI materials, synthetic biology, functional films, semiconductor packaging materials, fine-chemical monomers, and commodity plastics or petrochemical cases.

## 1.2 Retention Standard

1. Retain direct-watch companies with platform-level chemical new-material capability such as insulation/optical films, biomass/electronic resins, high-end ceramic materials, modified/special engineering plastics, and synthetic-biology materials.
2. Retain boundary cases when specialty polymers, EVA/PLA/UHMWPE, heat stabilizers, electromagnetic functional materials, thermal/EMI materials, wind/NEV resins, functional films, semiconductor packaging materials, or fine-chemical monomers have customer qualification and process know-how.
3. Reject commodity PET, nylon chips, oil inks, LPG processing, wood-plastic composites, ST fluorochemical cases, and mixed chemical/rail businesses when the edge is mainly capacity or cycle exposure.
4. For new materials, the central test is whether product formula, purity/process, customer qualification, application know-how, or platform breadth would remain hard to copy after a well-funded competitor enters.
