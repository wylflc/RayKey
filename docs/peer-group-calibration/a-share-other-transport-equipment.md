# 1. A-Share Other Transport Equipment Peer-Group Review

## 1.1 Purpose

This mixed group contains container and energy equipment, personal mobility, powersports, two-wheelers, rail signaling, bicycle brands, rail components, and niche electric vehicles. The review ignores the generic industry label and judges the real business moat of each company.

## 1.2 Retention Standard

1. Retain equipment platforms when global customer qualification, safety certification, manufacturing know-how, installed base, dealer network, or product engineering cannot be quickly bought by a new entrant.
2. Retain boundary cases when brand plus product technology creates a niche, but record competition, cycle, or scale limits.
3. Reject bicycle, ordinary two-wheeler, small electric tourist-vehicle, component, or weak follower cases when a well-funded entrant or stronger peer can copy the product and channel.

## 1.4 Sources

This first-pass peer-group review uses the local full-coverage score fields as background metadata and records official exchange disclosure landing pages in each decision row. Aggregator company introductions are not treated as decisive evidence. Later deep-company reviews should use annual reports, exchange announcements, official investor-relations materials, customer qualification evidence, product certifications, market-share evidence, segment margins, and reputable institutional research where needed.
