# 1. A-Share Other Metal New Materials Peer-Group Review

## 1.1 Purpose

This group contains metal-fiber, powder-metallurgy, magnesium/lightweighting, superconducting, titanium, superalloy, copper-alloy, master-alloy, and metal-composite companies. The review corrects baseline under-scoring where strategic material qualification, process know-how, or national-security relevance matters more than current-cycle profit.

## 1.2 Retention Standard

1. Retain direct-watch companies when they control scarce strategic-material capability, such as superconducting/titanium/superalloy platforms, global magnesium/lightweighting platforms, or differentiated metal-fiber technology.
2. Retain boundary cases when the company has credible powder-metallurgy, copper-alloy, master-alloy, aerospace alloy, titanium, metal-composite, or state-research-institute material capability that would not be easy for capital alone to replicate quickly.
3. Reject ordinary aluminum processing, mixed glass/material businesses, and small alloy manufacturers when stronger retained peers already cover the thesis or when category leadership is not visible.
4. For strategic materials, current profit cyclicality is background risk, not a sufficient reason to reject a company with verified qualification, process, or resource/manufacturing scarcity.
