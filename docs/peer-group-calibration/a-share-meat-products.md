# 1. A-Share Meat Products Peer-Group Review

## 1.1 Purpose

This group compares national processed-meat brands, traditional ham brands, casual braised-food chains, poultry/pork processors, and regional meat platforms. The review distinguishes consumer brand/channel moats from commodity protein processing.

## 1.2 Retention Standard

1. Retain direct-watch companies with national scale, brand trust, channel density, and full-chain processed-meat economics.
2. Retain boundary cases when a category brand or franchise network has meaningful consumer mindshare, while marking ST/governance risk for deeper review rather than using current profit alone.
3. Reject slaughtering, poultry/pork processing, regional meat platforms, and weaker braised-food followers when the business is mainly commodity protein capacity or lacks category leadership.
4. Food-safety trust, brand, channel and repeat consumption matter; simple processing capacity or farming integration is not a durable moat by itself.
