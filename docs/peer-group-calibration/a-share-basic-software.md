# 1. A-Share Basic Software Peer-Group Review

## 1.1 Purpose

This group compares office software, domestic operating-system/ecosystem software, databases, big-data platforms, font/IP software, and government/enterprise management software. The review emphasizes product depth, ecosystem lock-in, and data/software infrastructure rather than project delivery.

## 1.2 Retention Standard

1. Retain direct-watch companies when the product is a national-scale office, operating-system, or core software ecosystem with high switching costs.
2. Retain boundary cases when database, big-data/AI infrastructure, or software-IP assets are differentiated enough to resist simple capital replication.
3. Reject project-heavy enterprise/government software when the business looks more like customization and implementation than scalable product infrastructure.
4. For software, a watched company needs product reuse, ecosystem lock-in, standard/interface control, data model depth, or IP assets; manpower scale alone is insufficient.

## 1.4 Sources

This first-pass group review uses the local full-coverage score fields as background metadata and records official exchange disclosure landing pages plus official company websites in each decision row. Aggregator company introductions are not treated as decisive evidence. Later deep-company reviews should use annual reports, exchange announcements, official investor-relations materials, product certifications, customer qualification evidence, market-share evidence, segment margins, and reputable institutional research where needed.
