# 1. A-Share Rail Transit Equipment Peer-Group Review

## 1.1 Purpose

This group compares rail-control systems, traction/electrical systems, tunnel-boring equipment, rail doors, rail materials, inspection/monitoring, interiors, and rail supply-chain companies. The review separates safety-critical and system-level qualification moats from ordinary rail components.

## 1.2 Retention Standard

1. Retain direct-watch companies with national rail-control systems, traction/electrical systems, tunnel-boring or railway-construction machinery platforms, and safety-critical engineering equipment.
2. Retain boundary cases when rail doors, rail materials, inspection/monitoring, or signal-system niches have certification, installed-base, or customer switching-cost evidence.
3. Reject supply-chain/logistics companies, interiors, ordinary rail electrical parts, narrow onboard equipment, and weak rail-equipment stories without a safety-critical or system-level role.
4. Railway customer qualification is valuable, but a watched company must also control a critical product, system, data, or reliability role rather than just supply replaceable parts.

## 1.4 Sources

This first-pass group review uses the local full-coverage score fields as background metadata and records official exchange disclosure landing pages plus official company websites in each decision row. Aggregator company introductions are not treated as decisive evidence. Later deep-company reviews should use annual reports, exchange announcements, official investor-relations materials, product certifications, customer qualification evidence, market-share evidence, segment margins, and reputable institutional research where needed.
