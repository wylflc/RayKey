# 1. A-Share Apparel Peer-Group Review

## 1.1 Purpose

This low-barrier consumer group contains fashion brands, menswear, children's wear, underwear, shoes, outdoor, B2B uniforms, C2M manufacturing, and apparel exporters. The review allows broad rejection because ordinary apparel is easy to enter with capital and marketing spend.

## 1.2 Retention Standard

1. Retain direct-watch companies only when brand, category control, channel density, or consumer mindshare is meaningfully stronger than ordinary apparel peers.
2. Retain boundary cases when a company has differentiated intimate-apparel brands, high-end multi-brand fashion, medical-aesthetics cross-industry optionality, B2B uniform workflow, C2M manufacturing systems, or global manufacturing qualification that is not easily copied.
3. Reject ordinary fashion, menswear, shoes, exporters, old brands, ST cases, and weak e-commerce apparel when brand strength or process differentiation does not create durable pricing power.
4. For apparel, current rebound potential is not enough. A watched company must have durable brand/category control, channel/service workflow, or cross-industry capability that survives the next down cycle.

## 1.4 Sources

This first-pass group review uses the local full-coverage score fields as background metadata and records official exchange disclosure landing pages plus official company websites in each decision row. Aggregator company introductions are not treated as decisive evidence. Later deep-company reviews should use annual reports, exchange announcements, official investor-relations materials, product certifications, customer qualification evidence, market-share evidence, segment margins, and reputable institutional research where needed.
