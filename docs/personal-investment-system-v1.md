# Personal Investment System v1.0

> This document is a personal investing rulebook. It is not investment advice.

## 1. Core Principle

The system is:

```text
Value-investing foundation + industry-cycle research + right-side execution + position risk budgeting.
```

The operating rule is:

```text
Research decides what can be bought.
Right-side signals decide when to buy.
Risk budget decides how much can be bought.
Thesis change decides whether to add, reduce, or exit.
Cost basis must not decide position actions.
```

The account should pursue high-quality opportunities with controlled permanent-loss risk. It is not a pure buy-and-hold strategy, not pure trading, and not thematic speculation.

## 2. Account Structure

### 2.1 Position Layers

| Layer | Single-Stock Weight | Count | Total Weight | Purpose |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: | --- |
| Core Holding | 15%-25% | 2-3 | <=60% | Main high-conviction positions |
| Quasi-Core Holding | 4%-10% | 4-8 | <=35% | Important candidates with partial validation |
| Tactical Holding | 0.5%-3% | 5-10 | <=15% | Early participation, tracking, and event response |
| Cash / Reserve | - | - | 0%-20% | Optionality, risk control, and margin buffer |

Hard limits:

| Rule | Limit |
| --- | ---: |
| Single stock normal maximum | 25% |
| Single stock exceptional maximum | 30% |
| Same industry-chain exposure | 40% |
| Same broad industry exposure | 60% |
| Tactical holdings combined | 15% |
| Non-core distressed/value-trap exposure | 15% |

Core holdings can exceed 25% only when all conditions hold:

1. The company has healthy financials.
2. The industry trend is improving.
3. The thesis is supported by filings, orders, financial reports, or industry data.
4. The bear case is explicitly written.
5. A 30% mark-to-market drawdown would not force liquidation or psychological breakdown.
6. Latest financial, order, or industry evidence still supports the thesis.

### 2.2 Watchlist Layers

| Layer | Size | Meaning |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Full Watch Pool | 30-80 names | Worth long-term tracking |
| Priority Watch Pool | 10-20 names | Most relevant opportunities this month |
| Ready-to-Trigger Pool | 3-8 names | Research done, waiting for price/event trigger |
| Actual Holdings | 8-15 names | Current portfolio |
| Core Holdings | 2-3 names | Main account drivers |

Do not buy many 1% positions just for observation. Observation should mainly happen through the watch pool. Tactical holdings are only for names with active triggers.

## 3. Market Regime And Gross Exposure

### 3.1 Market State

Use the main target-market index, such as CSI 300 for A-shares, Hang Seng Index for Hong Kong, and S&P 500 / Nasdaq 100 for U.S. equities.

| State | Conditions |
| --- | --- |
| Strong Market | Index above 200-day moving average, 200-day average rising, market breadth improving, volume expanding, clear leading sectors |
| Weak Market | Index below 200-day moving average, 200-day average falling, weak breadth, no durable main theme |
| Range Market | Neither strong nor weak |

Confirm a state only after it persists for about 20 trading days. Market state controls aggressiveness and gross exposure. It does not automatically force selling of strong individual holdings.

### 3.2 Gross Exposure

| Market State | Equity Exposure | Preferred Strategy |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Strong Market | 75%-100% | Industry-chain breakout, growth leaders, high-conviction core |
| Range Market | 45%-75% | GARP, low valuation with catalyst, shareholder-return names, selective breakouts |
| Weak Market | 15%-45% | Cash-flow compounders, monopoly resources, high dividend, deep undervaluation with catalyst |

## 4. Leverage

Leverage is disabled during the first full year of system operation. After one year, leverage can be used only if actual records show acceptable drawdown and rule discipline.

### 4.1 Leverage Limits

| Market State | Gross Leverage |
| --- | ---: |
| Weak market | 0.5x-0.9x |
| Range market | 0.8x-1.1x |
| Structural bull market | 1.0x-1.2x |
| Exceptional strong market with high-conviction opportunity | 1.2x-1.3x |

For a concentrated personal portfolio, long-term leverage above 1.3x is not allowed.

### 4.2 Leverage Eligibility

Leverage can be used only for assets with:

1. High liquidity.
2. Stable financial quality.
3. Clear safety margin.
4. No dependence on a single order, customer, policy, or rumor.
5. No obvious financial red flags.
6. A stress test showing no forced liquidation if the portfolio drops 25%-30%.

Leverage is forbidden for:

1. Pure theme stocks.
2. Single-order or single-customer stories.
3. High-valuation growth stocks without current validation.
4. Distressed stocks with complex accounting.
5. Late-cycle cyclicals.
6. Stocks that already rose several times in a short period.
7. Small-cap illiquid stocks.

## 5. Stock Strategy Categories

Every stock must have one primary strategy tag before purchase. Selling rules follow the original tag unless a full re-underwriting is completed.

| Code | Strategy | Maximum Layer | Core Question |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| A | DCF / Cash-Flow Compounder | Core | Can it compound cash flow without heroic assumptions? |
| B | Cigar-Butt Deep Value | Quasi-Core | Is it cheap, real, and has a catalyst? |
| C | GARP Growth | Core | Is growth real, durable, cash-generating, and underpriced? |
| D | Industry-Chain Breakout | Core | Is the company in a true bottleneck with profit conversion? |
| E | Fallen Quality Leader | Core | Is the problem temporary rather than structural? |
| F | Monopoly Resource | Core | Is it resource advantage plus supply constraint, not just commodity beta? |
| G | Shareholder-Return Deep Value | Quasi-Core | Can cash, dividends, buybacks, or capital returns unlock value? |

### 5.1 A: DCF / Cash-Flow Compounder

Entry standards:

1. Simple and durable business model.
2. Stable free cash flow.
3. Long-term ROE / ROIC above industry average.
4. Manageable capital expenditure.
5. Pricing power or cost pass-through.
6. Stable or improving margins.
7. Rational capital allocation.
8. Future 5-10 years not likely to be structurally disrupted.
9. Base-case expected return remains attractive without valuation expansion.

Exit or downgrade:

1. ROIC declines structurally.
2. Industry demand enters structural decline.
3. Management starts low-return expansion.
4. Dividend or buyback discipline deteriorates.
5. Valuation prices in the next several years of growth.
6. Expected return becomes clearly inferior to other available opportunities.

### 5.2 B: Cigar-Butt Deep Value

Entry standards:

1. Market cap below conservative liquidation or asset value.
2. Assets are likely real.
3. Main business does not keep burning large cash.
4. Debt structure is clear.
5. No major related-party or governance risk.
6. There is a catalyst.

Conservative liquidation value:

```text
Cash + 0.8 * receivables + 0.5 * inventory + 0.3 * fixed assets - total liabilities
```

Accepted catalysts:

1. Buyback.
2. Special dividend.
3. Insider or controlling-shareholder purchase.
4. Asset disposal.
5. Spin-off or business sale.
6. Privatization.
7. Industry-cycle bottom.
8. Debt restructuring.
9. Management change.
10. Policy change.

Position limits:

| Case | Weight |
| --- | ---: |
| Transparent financials, net cash, clear buyback/dividend | 5%-10% |
| Average financials, some undervaluation | 3%-5% |
| Severe distress, mostly turnaround | 1%-3% |
| Complex accounting or governance risk | Do not buy |

Time stop: exit after 18-24 months if there is no catalyst progress.

### 5.3 C: GARP Growth

Entry standards:

1. Revenue growth comes from real demand and share gain, not only price cycle.
2. Market share is rising.
3. Gross margin is stable or improving.
4. Cash flow is improving with profit.
5. Competition is not rapidly worsening.
6. Growth does not depend on continuous financing.
7. Valuation does not fully price a perfect future.
8. At least one core financial metric has beaten expectations.

PEG is only a rough filter. The growth rate must be estimated from:

```text
industry space * market share * price trend * margin trend
```

Growth stock sell signals:

1. Revenue growth steps down.
2. Gross margin weakens continuously.
3. Sales expense rises without revenue growth.
4. Receivables or inventory grow abnormally.
5. Price war starts.
6. Addressable market is disproven.
7. Valuation requires perfect execution.

### 5.4 D: Industry-Chain Breakout

This is the main offensive strategy.

The opportunity must have three tickets:

| Ticket | Requirement |
| --- | --- |
| Demand Ticket | Downstream demand is real and funded |
| Bottleneck Ticket | The company is in a scarce supply or hard-to-expand link |
| Profit Ticket | The bottleneck can materially enter listed-company revenue and profit |

Evidence levels:

| Level | Evidence | Action |
| ---: | --- | --- |
| 0 | Forum, rumor, concept | Record only |
| 1 | Industry trend clear, company benefit unverified | Watch pool |
| 2 | Announcement, sample, cooperation, capacity plan | Tactical holding allowed |
| 3 | Customer validation, order, revenue beginning | Quasi-core allowed |
| 4 | Financial statement confirms revenue/margin/profit | Core upgrade allowed |
| 5 | Continued earnings beats and stronger moat | Core hold allowed |

Can chase only if:

1. Demand is still being revised upward.
2. Orders, customers, or product pricing keep improving.
3. Future profit expectations are rising faster than the stock price.
4. Current valuation is not extreme versus 2-3 year normalized earnings.
5. Supply-demand remains tight.
6. Competitors are not quickly catching up.

Cannot chase if:

1. There is only theme and no order.
2. There is only sample and no revenue path.
3. The stock price rose much more than earnings estimates.
4. Peers rise indiscriminately without differentiation.
5. Management is issuing shares or reducing holdings aggressively.
6. Valuation already prices extreme bull case.

### 5.5 E: Fallen Quality Leader

First classify the decline:

| Decline Type | Meaning | Action |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Valuation Reset | Business is fine, price was too expensive | Watch and wait for right-side setup |
| Earnings Hit | Business is hurt temporarily | Wait for earnings stabilization |
| Thesis Break | Moat or business model is permanently damaged | Avoid |

Entry standards:

1. Valuation is near historical low range.
2. Cash flow remains healthy.
3. Core business is not structurally broken.
4. Profit expectations stop being revised downward.
5. Management improves capital allocation.
6. Price stops making new lows.
7. Right-side volume and trend confirmation appear.
8. Policy or industry environment improves.

Do not buy merely because a high-quality company has fallen a lot.

### 5.6 F: Monopoly Resource

Entry standards:

1. Large and high-quality reserves.
2. Low position on industry cost curve.
3. Long reserve life.
4. Capacity growth or reserve replacement.
5. Supply constraint from resource, policy, environmental approval, or capital discipline.
6. Balance sheet is controllable.
7. Management has acquisition and development discipline.
8. Dividends or buybacks are meaningful.

Cycle test:

```text
Does the company remain profitable and cash-generative in the last industry trough?
```

Sell or downgrade when:

1. Commodity price enters extreme optimism.
2. Industry supply expands aggressively.
3. Company makes expensive peak-cycle acquisitions.
4. Policy caps profit.
5. Dividend discipline deteriorates.
6. Stock price fully prices bull case.

### 5.7 G: Shareholder-Return Deep Value

Entry standards:

1. Net cash is meaningful.
2. Main business does not burn large cash.
3. Market cap is below cash plus conservative operating-business value.
4. Debt is low.
5. Operating cash flow is positive.
6. Buyback, dividend, or special dividend is recurring or likely.
7. Governance is clear enough.
8. No continuous dilution.
9. No major related-party concern.

Position guide:

| Case | Weight |
| --- | ---: |
| Real cash, explicit buyback/dividend, stable business | 5%-10% |
| Weak business but not cash-burning | 3%-5% |
| Cash rich but governance weak | 0%-3% |
| Cash authenticity doubtful | Do not buy |

## 7. Scoring System

Use the score to rank research priority, not to auto-buy.

| Dimension | Weight |
| --- | ---: |
| Business model / moat | 15 |
| Industry space / cycle | 15 |
| Competitive position | 15 |
| Financial quality | 10 |
| Management / capital allocation | 10 |
| Valuation safety margin | 15 |
| Catalyst / right-side signal | 10 |
| Risk transparency | 10 |
| Total | 100 |

| Score | Action |
| ---: | --- |
| 85-100 | Core candidate |
| 75-84 | Quasi-core candidate |
| 65-74 | Tactical candidate |
| 50-64 | Zero-position watch |
| <50 | Remove or pause |

High score does not automatically mean large position. Position size also depends on valuation, market state, portfolio concentration, and leverage.

## 8. Valuation And Scenario Analysis

Core and quasi-core holdings must have bear/base/bull cases.

| Case | Required Content |
| --- | --- |
| Bear | What happens if demand, order, margin, valuation, or policy disappoints |
| Base | Normal execution and reasonable valuation |
| Bull | Demand, margin, and valuation surprise positively |

Minimum payoff rule:

```text
base-case upside / bear-case downside > 2
```

For high-volatility industry-chain stocks, require above 3. For stable cash-flow names, 1.5-2 can be acceptable.

## 9. Buy Rules

### 9.1 Universal Buy Checklist

A stock can be bought only if all are true:

1. It is in the watch pool.
2. Research card is completed.
3. Thesis is clear.
4. Bear/base/bull case is written.
5. Right-side signal exists, or valuation is extremely low with a catalyst.
6. Error condition is written.
7. Target position size is defined.
8. Add, reduce, and exit rules are defined.

### 9.2 Bottom-Reversal Buy

Applies to fallen leaders, low-valuation reversals, and cyclical bottoms.

Buy only when at least four of the following are true:

1. No new low for 3-6 weeks.
2. Price forms a higher low.
3. Price breaks above the 60-day moving average with volume.
4. Pullback does not break the breakout platform.
5. Sector improves together.
6. Latest financial report does not deteriorate further.
7. Buyback, order, policy, price, or margin catalyst appears.
8. Stock outperforms index and sector.

Build in three tranches:

| Tranche | Trigger | Size |
| --- | --- | ---: |
| 1 | Bottom-reversal conditions met | 1/3 of target |
| 2 | Pullback holds platform with lower volume | 1/3 of target |
| 3 | New high or second fundamental validation | 1/3 of target |

If price falls back into the platform for two trading days or breaks the previous low, exit the tactical tranche.

### 9.3 Breakout / Industry-Chain Buy

Applies to industry-chain breakout and strong growth.

Trigger:

1. Volume is at least 2x the 20-day average.
2. Price breaks a platform of at least 3 months, or reaches a 52-week high.
3. Sector and leading peers confirm.
4. The 24-hour research framework confirms demand, bottleneck, and profit conversion.

Position path:

| Stage | Evidence | Weight |
| --- | --- | ---: |
| Initial move | Sector starts, company stronger than peers | 1%-3% |
| Early validation | Announcement, sample, cooperation, capacity evidence | 3%-5% |
| Strong validation | Customer, order, revenue begins | 5%-10% |
| Financial validation | Profit, margin, and orders beat | 10%-20% |
| Long-term validation | Bottleneck persists and moat strengthens | Core size |

Do not wait for a perfect pullback. Do not buy full size before validation.

### 9.4 24-Hour Event Response

When a watch-pool stock suddenly rises with volume, answer within 24 hours:

1. Why did it rise?
2. Is it a company event or industry event?
3. Is the event real?
4. Is there announcement, filing, customer, or financial evidence?
5. Is the company in a bottleneck link?
6. What percentage of profit can this business contribute?
7. Was the profit elasticity previously underestimated?
8. Which company in the chain benefits the most?
9. Who is the leader?
10. Is valuation still acceptable versus future profit?

The answer must be one of:

1. Buy tactical position.
2. Parameterized wait.
3. Reject.

Parameterized wait must specify:

```text
Buy at price X, weight Y, within N trading days.
If no pullback and price makes a new high within N days, buy first tranche by breakout rule.
```

"Keep watching" is not an allowed conclusion after a real trigger.

## 10. Add Rules

Add only when thesis strengthens. Do not add merely because price falls.

Allowed add conditions:

1. Financial report beats.
2. Order or customer validation improves.
3. Gross margin improves.
4. Industry cycle improves.
5. Breakout pullback holds.
6. Competitive structure improves.
7. Valuation remains attractive.

Rules:

1. No averaging down without new evidence.
2. Each add must be smaller than or equal to the previous buy unless there is a full re-underwriting.
3. No add if portfolio concentration limit would be violated.

## 11. Reduce And Sell Rules

Reduce or sell based on thesis, valuation, risk, opportunity cost, catalyst status, and right-side failure.

### 11.1 Thesis Sell

Sell or downgrade if:

1. Orders do not materialize.
2. Customer validation fails.
3. Margins fail to improve.
4. Industry demand is disproven.
5. Competitors enter faster than expected.
6. Technology route changes.
7. Policy logic changes.
8. Management statements conflict with financial data.

### 11.2 Valuation Sell

Reduce if:

1. Current price already reflects bull case.
2. Future 2-3 year profit is fully priced.
3. Stock price rises much faster than earnings revisions.
4. The sector enters broad bubble behavior.
5. Good news no longer moves the stock.

### 11.3 Opportunity-Cost Sell

Switch only if the new opportunity score is at least 15 points higher than the old holding, or the old holding has a clear thesis weakening.

This prevents frequent style chasing.

### 11.4 Risk-Control Sell

Reduce if:

1. Single stock exceeds 30%.
2. Same industry chain exceeds 40%.
3. Same broad industry exceeds 60%.
4. Leverage exceeds the safe range.
5. Several holdings share the same hidden beta.

### 11.5 Catalyst-Completion Sell

After a catalyst occurs, re-underwrite the stock. Reduce if there is no next catalyst or no further earnings revision.

Catalysts include:

1. Policy announcement.
2. Order announcement.
3. Product launch.
4. Financial report.
5. Index inclusion.
6. M&A or restructuring completion.
7. Dividend or buyback execution.

### 11.6 Technical Failure Sell

For tactical holdings and industry-chain breakouts:

1. Price breaks the breakout platform.
2. Price falls below 60-day moving average and cannot recover.
3. Volume expands on decline.
4. Rebound volume weakens.
5. Sector leader starts to break down.
6. Company has no new fundamental evidence.

### 11.7 Governance / Financial Red-Flag Sell

Sell or exclude if serious:

1. Controlling shareholder fund occupation.
2. Complex related-party transactions.
3. Abnormal receivables.
4. Abnormal inventory.
5. Operating cash flow persistently below profit.
6. Auditor changes or modified opinion.
7. CFO changes frequently.
8. High shareholder pledge.
9. High-position financing.
10. Continuous insider selling.
11. Management explanation conflicts with data.

## 12. Profit-Taking Rule For Potential Tenbaggers

### 12.1 Principle

The idea is reasonable only under one condition:

```text
If the thesis remains valid and continues to be validated, profit-taking must be slow and partial so the account does not sell out of a long-duration compounder or tenbagger.
```

This rule does not apply when thesis is disproven, financial quality deteriorates, or governance risk appears. In those cases, the stock can and should be fully exited.

### 12.2 Profit-Taking Triggers

Do not sell only because the position is profitable. Start partial profit-taking only if at least one of the following is true:

1. Position weight exceeds planned maximum.
2. Valuation reflects bull case.
3. Stock price rises much faster than earnings revisions.
4. Industry-chain supply is about to loosen.
5. Commodity or sector sentiment reaches extreme optimism.
6. Technical protection signal is triggered.
7. Better opportunity appears with at least 15-point score advantage.

### 12.3 Slow Reduction Schedule

| Profit From Average Cost | Action If Thesis Still Valid |
| ---: | --- |
| +50% | No automatic sell; review position weight and thesis |
| +100% | If valuation or weight is stretched, sell 10%-15% of remaining shares |
| +200% | Sell 10%-15% of remaining shares if no earnings revision catches up |
| +300%-500% | For each additional 100% gain, sell at most 10%-15% of remaining shares |
| >+500% | Stop mechanical selling; use trend and thesis protection |
| 10x | Keep a permanent residual position unless thesis is broken |

Reduction constraints:

1. At least 20 trading days between two profit-taking reductions.
2. Each reduction sells a percentage of remaining shares, not original shares.
3. Do not reduce more than 25% of remaining shares in one month unless risk limits are violated.
4. Never sell the last residual position while thesis remains valid.

### 12.4 Residual Position

For a stock that once became a core holding and remains fundamentally valid:

| Stock Type | Residual Holding |
| --- | ---: |
| Cash-flow compounder | 5%-10% account weight or 20%-30% of original share count |
| Industry-chain breakout | 3%-8% account weight or 15%-25% of original share count |
| Monopoly resource | 5%-10% account weight if supply constraint remains |
| Fallen quality leader after recovery | 3%-8% account weight if expected return remains acceptable |
| Cigar-butt deep value | No mandatory residual; exit after value realization |

Cigar-butt and event-driven positions are exceptions. They should be sold after value realization unless the company is fully re-underwritten under another strategy tag.

### 12.5 Trend Protection After Large Gains

For large winners:

| Strategy | Protection Line |
| --- | --- |
| Cash-flow compounder | Monthly close below 12-month moving average: reduce core to quasi-core |
| Industry-chain breakout | Weekly close below 30-week moving average: reduce by half |
| Tactical holding | Breakout platform failure: exit |
| Resource stock | Commodity price extreme plus supply expansion: reduce |

This protects against giving back major gains while avoiding premature full exit.

## 13. Portfolio-Level Risk Rules

### 13.1 Drawdown Budget

| Account Drawdown From Peak | Action |
| ---: | --- |
| -8% | Remove leverage |
| -12% | Yellow warning: no upgrades, no new leverage, only add with strong evidence |
| -20% | Red warning: reduce gross exposure to <=50%, stop new buys for 10 trading days, complete review |

### 13.2 Single-Trade Risk

For tactical and right-side entries:

```text
position weight * stop-loss distance <= 1.5% of account NAV
```

Core holdings do not use simple price stop-loss, but they must trigger thesis review at -20% and require new evidence at -30%.

## 14. Workflow

### 14.1 Daily

Track only:

1. Current holdings.
2. Ready-to-trigger pool.
3. Top 10 priority watch names.
4. Major industry events.
5. Official announcements and financial reports.
6. Key industry prices, orders, policies, and capacity data.

### 14.2 Weekly

Answer:

1. Has any core thesis changed?
2. Can any quasi-core be upgraded?
3. Should any tactical position be removed?
4. Did any watch-pool stock trigger a buy signal?
5. Which scores rose?
6. Which scores fell?
7. Is the market strong, weak, or range-bound?
8. Is leverage too high?
9. Is industry exposure too concentrated?
10. What are next week's top 5 names to track?

### 14.3 Quarterly

After each reporting season, update:

1. Revenue.
2. Gross margin.
3. Net profit.
4. Operating cash flow.
5. Receivables.
6. Inventory.
7. Capex.
8. Management guidance.
9. Orders and backlog.
10. Difference versus thesis.

### 14.4 Annual

Review:

1. Return versus benchmark.
2. Maximum drawdown.
3. Win rate.
4. Payoff ratio.
5. Rule violations.
6. Missed large winners.
7. Biggest avoidable loss.
8. Whether active investing still adds value.

If the system underperforms the benchmark by more than 10 percentage points over four consecutive quarters, reduce active allocation or revise the system after a written review.

## 15. Forbidden Actions

1. Do not refuse to reduce a losing position because it is below cost.
2. Do not sell a winner only because it is profitable.
3. Do not use leverage for theme stocks.
4. Do not build a large position without financial or order validation.
5. Do not buy without bear/base/bull cases.
6. Do not treat the breakout starting price as a floor.
7. Do not chase because of regret.
8. Do not hold a tactical failure as a long-term investment.
9. Do not average down without new evidence.
10. Do not change rules during a loss week.

## 16. One-Vote Veto

Do not buy, or exit if already held, when severe:

1. Financial fraud suspicion.
2. Controlling shareholder fund occupation.
3. Complex related-party transactions.
4. Receivables grow far faster than revenue.
5. Inventory surges without revenue support.
6. Operating cash flow persistently lags profit.
7. Auditor replacement or modified audit opinion.
8. Continuous insider selling.
9. High-position financing after a price surge.
10. High goodwill from repeated acquisitions.
11. Core business keeps losing money without credible recovery.
12. More story than financial validation.
13. Only rumor, no announcement or customer validation.
14. Company has repeatedly chased hot themes without results.

## 17. Final Execution Summary

```text
Build a high-quality watch pool first.
Wait for right-side trigger before buying.
Enter in tranches.
Upgrade only after validation.
Do not average down without new evidence.
Reduce winners slowly if thesis remains valid.
Keep a residual position in true long-duration winners.
Exit immediately when thesis is broken.
Use market regime to control total exposure.
Use records and quarterly reviews to improve the system.
```
